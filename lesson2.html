Lesson 2: Morphing Proportion

Understanding proportion and manipulating elements to create surreal art

This lesson is inspired by Picasso's Bull, a series of drawings depicting a bull in decreasingly detailed form. As detail leaves and the bull becomes a combination of geometric shapes, proportion of different body parts begins to morph. In this lesson, you will attempt a similar series of exercises with an animal or person, simplifying the overall shape of the figure and changing the proportion of different body parts to each other. Creating surreal, fragmented interpretations of reality like this will open up a whole new branch of drawing technique for you which you can apply to many different types of art.
